The Monomer

On its own the protein has no fixed shape: 140 residues of intrinsically disordered chain, wandering. Only one stretch — the hydrophobic NAC — has any appetite to organise.

140 residues · N-terminal 1–60 (lipid-binding) · NAC 61–95 (the core-to-be) · C-terminal 96–140 (acidic, stays floppy)

The β-Strand

Inside an aggregate, the core flattens into an extended, pleated β-strand — the rigid plank from which every higher form is built.

core residues 70–88 · 19 residues · ≈ 60 Å end-to-end · ~3.85 Å rise per residue

The Parallel Slab

Strands stack like planks, all running the same way (N→C). The hydrogen bonds lie slanted between them. This is the grain of the mature fibril — three strands here make a three-rung slice of cross-β.

3 chains · neighbour spacing ≈ 5.3 Å (the cross-β signature) · activity score 16.3

The Antiparallel Slab

The same planks, but neighbours point in opposite directions. The hydrogen bonds now run straight across — a snugger weave that several studies report for toxic oligomers, distinct from the parallel fibril.

alternating N→C · straight H-bonds · activity score 13.7

The Relaxed Basin

Released into unbiased physics, the tidy slab will not hold. The strands curl, the sheet buckles, one edge peels open — yet it does not dissolve. It settles into a looser shape that is no longer the hand-built target but keeps much of its menace.

after 50 ns · edge opens 5 → 8 Å · β-core drift ~6 Å · activity 16.3 → 11.4 (floor of harmless ≈ 3.7)

The Funnel Test

Eight independent starts — different coil draws, both registers, two shifted β-cores, and a disordered coil — each relaxed for 50 ns, then mapped by mutual β-core distance. They do not fall into one shape. They split by register into a few history-dependent basins: the parallel starts gather around the hand-built target, the antiparallel form a separate basin, and disorder never folds. Repeating it at 2 and 4 chains doesn't tidy the picture — oligomer size shifts the toxic score non-monotonically without funnelling to one shape.

8 starts → 3 macro-basins · mean spread ≈ 14 Å · within a register independent starts converge; across registers they do not

The Fibril

The destination, if nucleation is crossed: thousands of strands stacked rung upon rung along a single axis, each layer one protein. To get here the slab must lock into in-register parallel cross-β and bury the NAC — a barrier many toxic oligomers never cross.

rung spacing 4.8 Å · width 5–10 nm · length 0.1–several µm (~2,000 rungs per micron)
