## Purpose

This is the first external-validation moment for the screen. The pre-registered hold-out ([#6](https://github.com/xag/asyn-oligomer-screen/issues/6)) shows the framework recovers *known* α-syn modulators; it does **not** show that novel predictions are real destabilisers in cells. This package hands 11 molecules — 7 novel protective candidates and 4 reactive metabolites — to a wet-lab partner for orthogonal-mechanism assays, turning sweep rankings into the project's two-sided deliverable: confirmed **what to do** (protective dietary / endogenous items) and confirmed **what to avoid** (reactive metabolites linked to diet, cooking, lifestyle).

**Receptor:** `fusco_parallel_3mer_core70-88_relaxed.pdb` (apo activity +17.78). **Channels:** `delta_activity_gated` (non-covalent rearrangement, −6 kcal/mol affinity-gated) for the protective set; `aspr_score` (covalent adduct propensity) for the reactive set.

### 1. Dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA)

- **Provenance:** novel — no prior α-syn evidence
- **Predicted mechanism:** Steroid scaffold docks into the exposed hydrophobic β-core groove of the oligomer; predicted to raise the toxic shape’s free energy.
- **Suggested assay:** ThT aggregation kinetics + DLS oligomer-size distribution on recombinant α-syn; dose–response from reported brain concentrations.
- **Delivery / actionable lever:** oral DHEA raises plasma; brain entry passive — Levels fall with age and are lower in Parkinson’s; regular exercise raises them, and it is sold as a supplement.
- **Brain access:** A fat-soluble hormone the body makes; passes into the brain, and a supplement raises brain levels. (typical brain level 1 nM–50 nM)

### 2. Retinoic acid (all-trans)

- **Provenance:** novel — no prior α-syn evidence
- **Predicted mechanism:** Nuclear-receptor ligand; here scored as a direct binder of the toxic shape independent of its transcriptional role.
- **Suggested assay:** ThT + DLS under amber light (photolabile); pair with all-trans/13-cis comparison.
- **Delivery / actionable lever:** dietary retinol or β-carotene → retinoic acid; therapeutic isotretinoin reaches CNS — Eat enough vitamin A (liver, eggs, dairy) or its plant form beta-carotene (orange/leafy veg).
- **Brain access:** The active form of vitamin A; the body makes it from dietary vitamin A and it is fat-soluble enough to enter the brain. (typical brain level 5 nM–50 nM)

### 3. Allopregnanolone

- **Provenance:** novel — no prior α-syn evidence
- **Predicted mechanism:** Neurosteroid; predicted direct binder of the hydrophobic groove, same scaffold class as DHEA.
- **Suggested assay:** ThT + DLS; precursor (pregnenolone) arm for the actionable lever.
- **Delivery / actionable lever:** oral pregnenolone raises plasma; allopregnanolone injection in clinical trials — The brain makes it from pregnenolone; chronic stress lowers it, so sleep and stress reduction help, and pregnenolone is sold as a supplement.
- **Brain access:** A fat-soluble brain steroid; gets in, and its precursor pregnenolone (taken by mouth) raises how much the brain makes. (typical brain level 1 nM–50 nM)

### 4. Δ9-Tetrahydrocannabinol (THC)

- **Provenance:** novel — no prior α-syn evidence
- **Predicted mechanism:** Lipophilic cannabinoid; scored as a direct binder. CB1/CB2 signalling is a separate, untested axis.
- **Suggested assay:** ThT + DLS in vitro (receptor-independent), so the binding hypothesis is testable without cannabinoid signalling.
- **Delivery / actionable lever:** oral, inhaled, sublingual; lipophilic, accumulates in brain
- **Brain access:** Fat-soluble, so it slips straight into the brain — the fact that it gets you high is direct proof it arrives there. (typical brain level 10 nM–500 nM)

### 5. Piperine

- **Provenance:** novel — no prior α-syn evidence
- **Predicted mechanism:** Black-pepper alkaloid; scored as a direct binder. Note the separate, well-known bioavailability-enhancer role.
- **Suggested assay:** ThT + DLS; clean food-compound chemistry, no special handling.
- **Delivery / actionable lever:** black pepper; reaches CNS and inhibits curcumin glucuronidation
- **Brain access:** The pungent compound in black pepper; gets into the brain itself, and also slows the gut/liver from clearing other compounds taken with it. (typical brain level 10 nM–500 nM)

### 6. Trehalose

- **Provenance:** novel — no prior α-syn evidence
- **Predicted mechanism:** Chemical chaperone / autophagy inducer. The reported in-vivo benefit is peripheral/autophagic — it does not reach the brain (see brain-access note).
- **Suggested assay:** In-vitro ThT + DLS still tests the direct-binding hypothesis; do NOT read a positive in-vitro result as a brain-delivery claim.
- **Delivery / actionable lever:** mushrooms, supplements; oral CNS entry limited but autophagy effects observed at oral doses
- **Brain access:** A sugar that the gut splits into glucose before absorption — almost none gets into the blood intact, and it cannot enter the brain. Its anti-clumping effect in animals works through the gut and body, not by reaching the brain. (typical brain level —)

### 7. Urolithin A

- **Provenance:** novel — no prior α-syn evidence
- **Predicted mechanism:** Gut metabolite of ellagitannins; mitophagy inducer with reported α-syn reduction in worm/cell models. Scored as a direct binder here.
- **Suggested assay:** ThT + DLS for the binding hypothesis; cell-based mitophagy readout for the orthogonal mechanism.
- **Delivery / actionable lever:** pomegranate/walnut ellagitannins + competent microbiome, OR direct urolithin A supplements (e.g. Mitopure) — Eat pomegranate, walnuts, and berries — but only ~40% of people have the gut bacteria to make it; the supplement (Urolithin A / Mitopure) works for everyone.
- **Brain access:** Made from pomegranate/walnut compounds by gut bacteria in only about 40% of people; only a little reaches the brain, and only if your gut makes it. (typical brain level 10 nM–500 nM)

### 1. Malondialdehyde (MDA)

- **Provenance:** novel — no prior α-syn evidence
- **Predicted mechanism:** Lipid-peroxidation dialdehyde; predicted Lys-Schiff cross-linking on the disordered N-tail.
- **Suggested assay:** ThT (does the adduct accelerate aggregation?) + LC-MS adduct mapping to localise the modified residues.
- **Source / exposure:** Not eaten as such — it forms inside brain tissue when fats are damaged by oxidation; the lever is lowering that oxidative damage.

### 2. Acrolein

- **Provenance:** documented
- **Predicted mechanism:** Most reactive common α,β-unsaturated aldehyde; Michael adducts cross-link α-syn and accelerate oligomerisation (published anchor).
- **Suggested assay:** ThT + LC-MS adduct mapping; positive control for the covalent channel given existing literature.
- **Source / exposure:** Forms inside the brain from fat and amine breakdown, and is also delivered directly by tobacco smoke and overheated cooking oils.

### 3. 4-Hydroxynonenal (4-HNE)

- **Provenance:** documented
- **Predicted mechanism:** ω-6 lipid-peroxidation aldehyde; Michael adducts on α-syn nucleophiles, elevated in PD substantia nigra (published anchor).
- **Suggested assay:** ThT + LC-MS adduct mapping; benchmark against published 4-HNE–α-syn adduct sites.
- **Source / exposure:** Forms inside the brain when omega-6 fats are oxidised; rises with oxidative stress, so the lever is antioxidant status and fat quality.

### 4. Methylglyoxal

- **Provenance:** documented
- **Predicted mechanism:** Reactive dicarbonyl; glycates and cross-links α-syn directly (published anchor; hyperglycaemia→aggregation bridge).
- **Suggested assay:** ThT + LC-MS adduct mapping (CEL/Schiff sites); glucose-load arm for the actionable lever.
- **Source / exposure:** A sugar-handling by-product made throughout the body, including the brain; it rises with high blood sugar, so the lever is glucose control.


## Assay guidance

- **Orthogonal mechanism is required (caveat [#29](https://github.com/xag/asyn-oligomer-screen/issues/29)).** A direct-binding-only assay cannot confirm a destabiliser whose mechanism is conformational or autophagic. Use readouts that detect aggregation/oligomer-state change (ThT kinetics, DLS), not just binding.
- **Protective set:** ThT aggregation kinetics and/or DLS oligomer-size distribution on recombinant α-syn, dose–response across the reported brain-concentration band.
- **Reactive set:** ThT **plus** LC-MS adduct mapping to localise the covalent modification (predicted Lys-CEL / Lys-Schiff / Michael adducts on the disordered N-tail).
- **Start with the first-pass trio** (DHEA, piperine, urolithin A) for supply / regulatory ease, then expand.

## Caveats carried into the handoff

- **Polyphenol bias is partly framework-coupled** (caveat [#24](https://github.com/xag/asyn-oligomer-screen/issues/24)) — the novel non-polyphenol hits carry more signal per rank than the polyphenol confirmatory band.
- **Scores are ordinal, not magnitude-interpretable** (caveat [#27](https://github.com/xag/asyn-oligomer-screen/issues/27)) — read the rank order, not the absolute Δact.
- **Δact is sign-bound by static-pose features** (caveat [#23](https://github.com/xag/asyn-oligomer-screen/issues/23)) — the screen currently sees destabilisers only.
